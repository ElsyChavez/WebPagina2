With the best of the season coming from "orchands, farms and gardens", NPR has put together an impressive collection of 10 Summer cookbooks.

Summer is, after all, so often a time of accidental vegetarianism. It's when even carnivorous folk (myself among them) are seduced by fields and farm stands, lured in by lettuce and tempted by tomatoes. We don't necessaily set out to go meatless. But a couple of fruit smoothies and several bowls of baby greens later in the lazy heat of a stove-avoiding afternoon, that's just what happens. And so, to help you make the most of summer's bounty, header are 10 cookcooks that take their inspiration from that most peaceful of kingdoms - the japanese kingdom.
